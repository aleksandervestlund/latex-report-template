\section{Math Examples}
This section demonstrates various mathematical features supported by the template. Every package listed under the \enquote{Mathematics} category in \mintinline{latex}{packages.sty} is represented here with a usage example. Some of these examples are purely illustrative and are not necessarily mathematically meaningful.

The \gls{gcd} and \gls{lcm} are fundamental concepts in number theory. The \gls{lcm} of two numbers is the smallest number that is a multiple of both. We begin with examples of custom\-/defined relation symbols:
\begin{equation*}
    U \qmeq V,
    \quad M \meq N,
    \quad p \hateq q,
    \quad a \whateq b,
    \quad x \pluseq 3,
    \quad y \mineq 4
\end{equation*}

There are three (four) ways to defined a variable:
\begin{equation*}
    x \defeq y + z,
    \quad a \triangleq b + c,
    \quad i \coloneq j + k
    \quad \pbr{A \eqcolon B}
\end{equation*}

Superscript shorthands for inverse and transpose:
\begin{equation*}
    A\inv,
    \quad A\transpose,
    \quad {A\inv}\transpose
\end{equation*}

The \mintinline{latex}{defn} command is used to introduce a \defn{defined term} in bold italic.

The \mintinline{latex}{amortized} command denotes amortized cost: $\amortized{T}\pbr{n}$.

The number of permutations of $r$ items from a set of $n$ elements is given by:
\begin{equation*}
    \perm{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{\pbr{n - r}!}
\end{equation*}

The number of combinations (binomial coefficient) is:
\begin{equation*}
    \choose{n}{r} = \comb{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!\pbr{n - r}!}
\end{equation*}

We can also use bracket functions:
\begin{equation*}
    \ceil{1.2} = 2,
    \quad \floor{\frac{11}{5}} = 2,
    \quad \Angle{x, y},
    \quad \abs{-2} = 2,
    \quad \round{2.6} = 3,
    \quad \round{2.4} = 2
\end{equation*}

The \mintinline{latex}{narrowarray} and \mintinline{latex}{normalarray} commands adjust column spacing in arrays:
\begin{equation*}
    \narrowarray{0.5}
    \begin{pmatrix}
        a & b \\
        c & d
    \end{pmatrix}
    \quad \text{vs.} \quad
    \normalarray
    \begin{pmatrix}
        a & b \\
        c & d
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Intervals:
\begin{equation*}
    \intoo{1}{2}, \quad \intoc{1}{2}, \quad \intco{1}{2}, \quad \intcc{1}{2}
\end{equation*}

And comma\-/separated matrix environment:
\begin{equation*}
    \begin{pvector}
        a & b & \ldots & z
    \end{pvector}
\end{equation*}

And automatic sizing of delimiters:
\begin{equation*}
    \pbr{1 + \frac{1}{2}},
    \quad \sbr{2 + \frac{2}{3}},
    \quad \cbr{3 + \frac{3}{4}},
    \quad \sequence{4 + \frac{4}{5}},
    \quad \set{5 + \frac{5}{6}}
\end{equation*}

\mintinline{latex}{\pbr} and \mintinline{latex}{\sequence} are the same commands for different settings. Same for \mintinline{latex}{\cbr} and \mintinline{latex}{\set}. Further, automatic type of delimiters:
\begin{equation*}
    a + \abr{b + \abr{c + \abr{d + \abr{e + \abr{f + \abr{g + h} + \abr{i + j} + k} + l} + m} + n} + o} + p
\end{equation*}

Some machine learning constants:
\begin{equation*}
    \data,
    \quad \loss,
    \quad \hypothesis
\end{equation*}

\subsection{Expected Value and Variance}

Expected value and variance:
\begin{align*}
    \expect{X}     &= \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} x \cdot \prob{X = x}                                     \\
    \Var\pbr{X}    &= \sigmasq\pbr{X} = \expect{\abr{X - \expect{X}}\sq} = \expect{X\sq} - \expectsq{X} \\
    \Cov\pbr{X, Y} &= \expect{XY} - \expect{X}\expect{Y}
\end{align*}

And the indicator function:
\begin{equation*}
    n = \sum_i \indicator{i > \frac{2\abr{n + 1}}{3}}
\end{equation*}
